Bio

Han Liu is a recent Ph.D. graduate in computer science from the University of Chicago, where he was a member of the Chicago Human+AI Lab (CHAI Lab), led by Prof. Chenhao Tan. His current research focuses on human–AI collaboration, delving into critical areas such as AI explainability, alignment, and machine teaching.

Fascinated by the relationship between humans and AI, Han explores how they can interact and collaborate. He envisions a future where humans harness the power of AI to tackle the most significant challenges in the world.

He earned his Bachelor's degree in mathematics, computer science, and linguistics at Washington University in St. Louis, where he cultivated a deep interest in artificial intelligence and natural language processing. Before moving to Chicago, he also spent a year at the University of Colorado Boulder as a graduate student.
